Corporate Governance

As Nigerian and African businesses expand across markets and deepen capital structures, rigorous governance becomes a competitive advantage. Our Corporate Governance practice guides boards, management, and shareholders through the complex interplay of company law, regulatory compliance, and commercial strategy.

We advise on incorporation, constitution drafting, director and officer duties, shareholder agreements, board restructuring, and compliance with the Companies and Allied Matters Act (CAMA) 2020. We help multinationals tailor group governance to Nigerian legal requirements, counsel emerging companies on setting best practices early, and navigate disputes between shareholders or within management structures with pragmatism and discretion.

In a market where corporate governance remains a key focus for institutional investors and regulators alike, our advice ensures your firm operates with transparency, reduces liability, and builds stakeholder confidence.

Services
  • Constitutional and memorandum of association drafting
  • Director and officer compliance and liability management
  • Shareholder dispute resolution and mediation
  • Board structuring and succession planning
  • CAMA 2020 compliance and group restructuring
  • Regulatory reporting and corporate record keeping

Recent Matter

Advised a Nigerian fintech unicorn on restructuring its holding company and subsidiary structure across Nigeria, Kenya, and Singapore to optimise tax efficiency while complying with local corporate governance and regulatory requirements.

Mergers & Acquisitions

In Nigeria's dynamic corporate landscape, M&A requires both international sophistication and acute local insight. We have spent nearly three decades advising buyers, sellers, and financial sponsors on transactions ranging from ₦500 million to ₦500 billion in value — across energy, financial services, telecommunications, fast-moving consumer goods, and technology sectors.

Our M&A practice covers all phases: target identification and valuation, due diligence, deal structure, tax and regulatory strategy, contract negotiation, and post-closing integration. We navigate the peculiarities of Nigerian commercial law, manage Central Bank approvals and sector-specific clearances, and advise on foreign exchange and repatriation requirements. We are equally comfortable with statutory mergers, share acquisitions, asset deals, and complex multi-jurisdictional carve-outs.

Our reputation and relationships—with regulators, financial advisors, and sponsors—mean faster deal execution and fewer surprises. We know Nigeria's M&A market and deliver candid, practical counsel rooted in your business priorities.

Services
  • Buy-side and sell-side M&A advisory
  • Due diligence (legal, commercial, regulatory)
  • Deal structuring and negotiation
  • Post-closing integration and representation matters
  • Regulatory approval management (CBN, FIRS, sector regulators)
  • Management buyouts and cross-border transactions

Recent Matter

Advised the lead sponsor and financial advisor on the acquisition of a leading Nigerian FMCG group valued at ₦45 billion, including full due diligence, regulatory submissions to FIRS for pioneer status approval, and shareholders' agreement negotiation.

Energy & Resources

Nigeria's energy sector remains central to the nation's economic future—and extraordinarily complex legally. We represent oil and gas majors, independent producers, service companies, renewable energy developers, and government bodies in transactions, disputes, regulatory matters, and project financing spanning upstream, midstream, and downstream sectors.

Our energy practice is rooted in deep knowledge of the Petroleum Industry Act (PIA), NNPC structures, host community agreements, environmental and regulatory compliance (DPR, NESREA, FIRS approvals), and the full spectrum of commercial and technical agreements governing onshore and offshore operations. We advise on production sharing contracts, gas supply and handling agreements, joint venture agreements, and financing structures. We have particular expertise in renewable energy compliance, community relations protocols, tax incentives, and disputes resolution.

Whether navigating the post-PIA landscape, structuring host community development agreements, managing NNPC engagement, or resolving contract disputes in international arbitration, our team brings sector authority and practical solutions to Nigeria's most challenging energy challenges.

Services
  • Production sharing contract negotiation and compliance
  • Host community agreements and stakeholder engagement
  • Petroleum Industry Act (PIA) compliance
  • Environmental and regulatory permitting
  • Energy project financing and security agreements
  • Dispute resolution and international arbitration

Recent Matter

Represented a major international oil company in negotiating and structuring a complex host community development agreement for a deepwater project, including NNPC engagement, NESREA compliance, and security arrangements. Matter exceeded ₦10 billion in community investment commitments.

Banking & Finance

From project financing to syndicated lending, debt restructuring to derivative products, Nigeria's banking and financial services sector is increasingly sophisticated—and legally demanding. We advise leading financial institutions, asset managers, microfinance banks, private equity funds, and large corporate borrowers on the full spectrum of banking and finance transactions.

Our practice encompasses term loan and revolving credit facilities, trade finance, structured finance, Islamic finance, guarantee and security documentation, collateral management, regulatory compliance (CBN capital requirements, AML/CFT), and workout and enforcement of security. We bring expertise in syndication structures, subordination hierarchies, financial covenants, project finance for infrastructure and energy, and cross-border financing structures optimised for Nigerian tax and regulatory requirements.

Whether structuring a complex project lender consortium, managing a difficult debt workout, or navigating CBN regulatory changes, we deliver counsel that serves the commercial interests of lenders and borrowers alike while ensuring regulatory compliance.

Services
  • Syndicated lending and facility agreements
  • Project finance and structured finance
  • Security documentation and enforcement
  • CBN regulatory compliance and licensing
  • Debt restructuring and workouts
  • Islamic finance (Shariah-compliant products and documentation)

Recent Matter

Advised a consortium of five international banks and a Nigerian development bank on a ₦280 billion syndicated term loan for a major telecommunications infrastructure project, including security trustee arrangements, subordination agreements, and CBN approval coordination.

Real Estate & Property

Real estate in Nigeria—whether commercial, residential, industrial, or mixed-use—is a high-value asset class fraught with title complications, regulatory overlays, and contract timing challenges. We advise developers, institutional investors, financiers, and occupiers on acquisitions, disposals, leasing, development agreements, and financing of property transactions.

Our real estate practice is grounded in expertise with Land Use Act 1978 issues, CAMA compliance, foreign direct investment approvals, property title verification and remediation, lease documentation (residential, commercial, industrial, agricultural), development and concession agreements, and real estate project financing. We navigate local government relations protocols, environmental clearances, planning approval processes, and dispute resolution in customary land disputes. We advise on Nigerian Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs), Build-Operate-Transfer (BOT) concessions, and public-private partnerships in real estate.

As Nigeria's real estate sector matures and foreign capital seeks entry, our counsel ensures transactions are legally sound from a Nigerian perspective, title is clear, finance structures are enforceable, and landlord-tenant relationships are documented with commercial clarity.

Services
  • Property acquisitions and disposals
  • Lease documentation and landlord/tenant matters
  • Title verification and remediation (Land Use Act issues)
  • Development and concession agreements
  • Real estate project financing
  • Foreign investment approvals and REIT structuring

Recent Matter

Advised a South African REIT on the acquisition of a ₦35 billion commercial real estate portfolio in Lagos and Abuja, including title verification across 12 properties, FIRS clearances, Land Use Act compliance, and structured lease documentation for institutional tenants.

Dispute Resolution & Litigation

Disputes are inevitable in complex transactions and long-term business relationships. When they occur, litigation or arbitration strategy becomes mission-critical. Our Dispute Resolution practice represents corporate clients, financial institutions, and individuals in civil litigation in Nigerian federal and state courts, commercial arbitration before domestic and international tribunals, mediation and alternative dispute resolution, and cross-border enforcement of judgments and awards.

We bring trial experience in the Federal High Court and Court of Appeal, arbitration experience under UNCITRAL, ICC, LCIA, and Lagos Court of Arbitration rules, and expertise in urgent applications, injunctions, and interlocutory matters. We understand commercial litigation dynamics—cost-benefit analysis, settlement strategy, evidence development, and court dynamics in Nigeria.

Whether representing a multinational in a ₦5 billion commercial dispute, defending a financial institution against regulatory enforcement action, or advising a company on pre-litigation strategy, our approach combines commercial pragmatism with courtroom advocacy. We resolve disputes efficiently while protecting long-term client interests.

Services
  • Federal High Court commercial litigation
  • Domestic and international commercial arbitration
  • Urgent applications and interlocutory relief
  • Mediation and alternative dispute resolution
  • Cross-border asset recovery and enforcement
  • White-collar defence and regulatory investigations

Recent Matter

Represented a major international corporation in a ₦12 billion LCIA arbitration against a Nigerian contractor over performance of a supply agreement. Achieved a favourable interim award, subsequent settlement at materially improved valuation, and enforcement of settlement in London High Court.
